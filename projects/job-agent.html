AI Job Agent

A personal project: scrape jobs from global boards, filter by my criteria, and auto-match them to my resume with DeepSeek AI — all logged to a Google Sheet.

What It Does

This is a personal project — a job scraper and matcher I built to cut through the noise and focus on roles that actually fit my background. Here’s what it does:

  • Runs automatically via GitHub Actions
  • Scrapes jobs from global boards via JSearch API (Rapid, acquired by Nokia)
  • Filters by my criteria (location, publish time, keywords in title and description)
  • Auto-matches each job to my resume using DeepSeek AI via OpenRouter
  • Scores fit and writes results into a Google Sheet

How It Evolved

In the first week I had an agent that scraped job listings with my filters and funneled them into a Google Sheet. It helped — but I still had to manually read and evaluate each one.

Then I gave it a brain. Now each job goes through DeepSeek, which compares the description to my resume and experience, returns a confidence score (e.g. 78% match), explains why it’s a good or bad fit, and suggests what I could improve. All of that gets logged into the sheet, so I can prioritize by fit instead of guesswork.

In practice it’s my daily dashboard: a live, ranked list of jobs tailored to me.

Time difference

Before: I was spending several hours a week scanning boards and reading through listings to see what fit. Now I spend much less time on that — the agent does the first pass, and I focus on the scored, ranked list in the sheet.

Why It Matters

There are thousands of listings, but only a handful truly align with your background. This project is about shifting from overwhelm to focus — using AI not just to scrape, but to curate smartly.
