About

Product strategist turned AI builder.

I started my career in financial services at JPMorgan Chase and NewDay, working across credit products, risk models, and customer analytics behind them. A lot of that work sat at the boundary between business and technology: making sense of data, designing product strategies through data analysis, writing requirements that engineers could actually use, and making sure insights and decisions reached the right people.

More recently at AlixPartners, I've been working on AI and analytics initiatives. This includes helping with internal AI adoption programs, delivering training on LLM tools and platforms like Databricks, and working with different teams to build technical solutions. I've also spent a lot of time speaking with cross-functional people to understand operational pain points and turning those into automation opportunities, building tools and workflows using technologies such as Microsoft Power Automate, AI agents, and custom internal applications.

Alongside that work, I became increasingly curious about what AI could do for everyday decision-making and operational workflows. So I started building LLM integrations, workflow automations, and full-stack prototypes, often based on real problems I encountered in practice.

I'm particularly interested in roles where product thinking and hands-on building both matter. What motivates me is creating AI tools that are genuinely useful for real organizations and users — improving how teams work and how decisions get made, not just building something technically impressive.
